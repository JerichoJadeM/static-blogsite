How to self-study

by Andrew V. Murray

Acquiring good study habits can make you a more effective, active leaner, whether in school, or in a job. Developing a plan will enable you to make the best use of your time for studying.

A good study plan includes setting up a study area, establishing a study schedule and using an assignment notebook. Your study area should be well lighted and free of distractions. It should be equipped with materials you need to work, such as pens, paper, ruler, eraser, books, computer if you can afford it.

Study periods must be set for times when you are most alert. Generally, study periods must be around 45 - 50 minutes each, unless when you have to study longer, especially during examination days. Take 10 to 15 minute breaks between study periods. Have time to visit libaries in school.

Have an assignment notebook where you can record short-term assignments like the daily homeworks, and projects such as researc papers. As to projects, divide them into short-term goals that must be met in order to complete the work.

Get the most from your Readings

A scholar once said that there are books that should only be "tasted," and books that should be "chewed and digestet".

So many books and reading materials including PDF files you downloaded from the internet or articles you read come out every day that no matter how long you read you cannot take them all in. That's why some materials should be just skimmed through, or read lightly and fast, materials you think are not so important or interesting.

Important and difficult materials should be read slowly and deeply. As you read, you also think, analyze and critize.

Concentrate

When you are reading to remember, as in the case of your lessons, concentration is very important. That is why studying in front of television is a bad thing. It is difficult to do two things at a time.

If you really want to concentrate on what you are going to study, do not eat a heavy meal because you will feel drowsy and probably fall asleep while reading.

Go for the meaning and answer to your questions. A few minutes of concentration is definitely better than an hour of half attention. Look for main idea in every paragraph, which is usually given in the key sentence. Most often it is the first sentence of the paragraph, but it may also be the last or any middle sentence.

Talk about the important and interesting things that you have read. This will help you remember them more and your knowledge will also be enriched by other people's reaction. Learn the meaning of the words that you do not know.

To increase your speed in reading, read with your eyes - remember, no sound no lip movement. You read aloud when you are practicing for an oral speech or oral reading, or when you are trying to memorize something.

Finally

Choose what you read. Don't read trash, Don't read materials that will poison or pollute your minds.

Reading is such an interesting and educational activity and hobby that you should try to develop your fondness for it. It entertains you, improves your vocabulary, and ability to understand, takes you to different places, introduces you to great people, give you so much information and knowledge.
